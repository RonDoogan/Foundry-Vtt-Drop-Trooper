DROP TROOPER v1.3 – UPDATED CORE SYSTEM
MASTER RULES FILE

Drop Trooper is a fast, tactical sci-fi combat game built for squad-level action and easy VTT play.
The system uses d6 dice pools. Only 6s count as successes on attack and defense rolls.
Damage dice are rolled normally and added together.

This version keeps the v1.2 core engine and adds the current working tactical actions, next-roll modifiers, smoke rules, sensor sweep rules, flight-pack mishaps, defeated state guidance, and group-fire guidance used in the Foundry build.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. CORE RESOLUTION
------------------------------------------------------------

Most combat attacks follow this sequence:

1. Roll Attack Dice
2. Determine Damage Dice
3. Target Rolls Defense Dice
4. Roll Remaining Damage Dice
5. Apply Armor Tier Multiplier
6. Apply Critical or Epic modifiers
7. Apply damage to Integrity, then Health

Other rolls such as skill, attribute, defense, repair, and ability checks use the same dice-pool logic, but do not follow the full damage sequence.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. ACTION ECONOMY
------------------------------------------------------------

Each trooper gets 2 actions on their turn.

Common actions include:
- Move
- Shoot
- Reload
- Aim
- Brace
- Use item
- Use ability
- Interact with objective
- Deploy gear or drone

Example turns:
- Move + Shoot
- Reload + Shoot
- Move + Reload
- Shoot + Shoot
- Aim + Shoot
- Brace + Move

The GM may restrict duplicate actions if a specific situation or weapon calls for it.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. ATTACK ROLL
------------------------------------------------------------

Roll Attack Dice (d6) based on weapon, ability, or effect.
Each 6 = 1 success.

Success Results:
- 0 successes = Miss
- 1 success = Hit
- 2 successes = +1 damage die
- 3 successes = Critical Hit
- 4+ successes = Epic Hit

If a temporary modifier reduces an attack pool to 0 dice, the attack becomes an automatic miss.
The modifier is still consumed.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. DAMAGE DICE
------------------------------------------------------------

Each weapon has a base damage dice pool.

Examples:
- Rifle – 4d6
- Heavy Rifle – 5d6
- Missile Launcher – 8d6

Attack successes may modify damage:
- 1 success: roll base damage
- 2 successes: add +1 damage die
- 3 successes: Critical Hit
- 4+ successes: Epic Hit

Damage dice are separate from attack dice.
A modifier that affects the next roll does not automatically affect damage unless a specific rule says it does.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. DEFENSE ROLL
------------------------------------------------------------

After a hit is confirmed, the target may roll Defense Dice (d6).
Each 6 removes 1 damage die from the incoming attack.

Defense represents:
- armor maneuvering
- shield systems
- reactive thrusters
- evasive movement
- cover

Defense can reduce damage dice to 0.
If all damage dice are removed, the attack deals 0 damage.

Defense does not turn a hit into a miss. It only reduces or negates damage.

If a temporary modifier reduces a defense pool to 0 dice, the defense roll becomes an automatic fail and removes 0 damage dice.
The modifier is still consumed.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. DAMAGE ROLL
------------------------------------------------------------

Roll the remaining damage dice and total the result.

Example:
4d6 = 4 + 6 + 3 + 5 = 18 damage

If defense reduced the pool to 0 dice, the attack deals 0 damage.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. ARMOR TIERS AND DAMAGE MULTIPLIERS
------------------------------------------------------------

Weapons and armor both have tiers.

Armor / Weapon Tiers:
- Light  – creatures, recon armor, light weapons
- Medium – combat armor, standard troops, military rifles
- Heavy  – battlesuits, tanks, heavy weapons
- Ultra  – starship scale systems and ship armor

Compare the weapon tier to the target's armor tier.

Damage Multiplier:
- Same tier = x1 damage
- Weapon 1 tier higher = x2 damage
- Weapon 2+ tiers higher = x4 damage
- Weapon 1 tier lower = x0.5 damage
- Weapon 2+ tiers lower = x0.25 damage

Examples:
- Heavy weapon vs Heavy armor = x1
- Heavy weapon vs Medium armor = x2
- Heavy weapon vs Light armor = x4
- Light weapon vs Medium armor = x0.5
- Light weapon vs Heavy armor = x0.25

Round final damage up after applying multipliers unless the result is 0.

------------------------------------------------------------
8. CRITICAL HITS
------------------------------------------------------------

If the attack roll produces exactly 3 successes, the hit is Critical.

Resolve the attack normally, then:
- Total Damage x2

Critical hits do not automatically add narrative effects unless the GM wants to.
Their core effect is doubled damage.

------------------------------------------------------------
9. EPIC HITS
------------------------------------------------------------

If the attack roll produces 4 or more successes, the hit is an Epic Hit.

Resolve the attack normally, then:
- Total Damage x2

In addition, the GM may apply one cinematic effect that fits the weapon and situation.
Examples include:
- knockback
- armor breach
- weapon damage
- stagger
- environmental destruction
- system disruption

Epic Hits are meant to feel dramatic, dangerous, and memorable.

------------------------------------------------------------
10. ARMOR INTEGRITY AND HEALTH
------------------------------------------------------------

Armor absorbs damage before the trooper is injured.

Damage is applied in this order:
Damage -> Armor Integrity -> Health

When Integrity reaches 0, any remaining damage is applied to Health.
Once Integrity is gone, future damage goes directly to Health unless armor is repaired.

Example Integrity Values:
- Recon Armor – 18
- Combat Armor – 20
- Heavy Battlesuit – 24

------------------------------------------------------------
11. DEFEATED STATE
------------------------------------------------------------

When a unit's Health is reduced to 0, that unit is defeated.

Defeated means:
- the unit is out of the fight
- the GM may remove it, leave it in place as a wreck or body, or treat it as incapacitated depending on the scenario
- defeated is separate from armor break; Integrity 0 only means armor has failed, not that the unit is defeated

------------------------------------------------------------
12. ARMOR TYPES
------------------------------------------------------------

Armor type matters because it determines the tier used in damage scaling.

Common armor types:

Recon Armor
- Tier: Light
- Example Integrity: 18
- Fast, mobile, vulnerable to heavier weapons

Combat Armor
- Tier: Medium
- Example Integrity: 20
- Standard military protection

Heavy Battlesuit
- Tier: Heavy
- Example Integrity: 24
- Slower, tougher, resistant to lighter weapons

Ship Armor
- Tier: Ultra
- Used for starfighters, gunships, dropships, and larger vessels

------------------------------------------------------------
13. AMMO SYSTEM
------------------------------------------------------------

Most ranged weapons track ammunition.

Weapons list:
- Ammo Current
- Magazine Size
- Reserve Ammo

Example:
Pulse Rifle
Ammo Current: 12
Magazine Size: 12
Reserve Ammo: 24

When a weapon is fired, reduce Ammo Current as appropriate.

If Ammo Current reaches 0, the weapon cannot be fired until reloaded unless a special rule says otherwise.

------------------------------------------------------------
14. RELOAD ACTION
------------------------------------------------------------

Reloading costs 1 action.

When a trooper reloads:
- Refill the weapon up to its Magazine Size
- Remove that amount from Reserve Ammo
- A trooper cannot reload more ammo than remains in Reserve

Example:
Magazine Size 12
Ammo Current 3
Reserve Ammo 14

Reloading loads 9 rounds.
New totals:
Ammo Current 12
Reserve Ammo 5

A trooper with 2 actions may Reload + Shoot on the same turn.

------------------------------------------------------------
15. AOE WEAPONS AND BLAST ZONES
------------------------------------------------------------

Explosive weapons create blast zones.
Examples include:
- grenades
- missile launchers
- satchel charges
- orbital strikes

Standard blast zones:
- Kill Zone = full damage dice
- Heavy Splash = half damage dice
- Fragmentation = quarter damage dice

Each target in the blast rolls defense normally.

For reduced blast dice:
- Half dice: round up
- Quarter dice: round up
- Minimum 1 die if the target is still inside the blast area

Example:
Missile Launcher base damage = 8d6
- Kill Zone: 8d6
- Heavy Splash: 4d6
- Fragmentation: 2d6

The GM may assign exact distances for each zone by weapon.

Example missile profile:
- 0–30 ft = Kill Zone
- 31–60 ft = Heavy Splash
- 61–100 ft = Fragmentation

Some explosives may also have a minimum arming distance.

------------------------------------------------------------
16. SMOKE GRENADES
------------------------------------------------------------

Smoke grenades use the same blast-style aiming flow as other AOE weapons.

Smoke Grenade Rules:
- Uses AOE targeting to choose the landing point
- Creates a 30 ft diameter smoke area
- Lasts 3 rounds
- Deals no damage unless a separate weapon rule says otherwise
- The GM may remove the smoke early if an effect clears it

Smoke Effects:
- Smoke blocks line of sight through it
- A unit inside smoke cannot see clearly out through the smoke unless the GM rules otherwise
- A unit on one side of smoke cannot see clearly through it to targets on the far side
- Smoke blocks Sensor Sweep and similar battlefield scanners unless a future ability specifically says it can penetrate smoke
- Smoke is a battlefield control tool and should be treated as hard concealment and sensor interference, not a damage effect

------------------------------------------------------------
17. SENSOR SWEEP
------------------------------------------------------------

Sensor Sweep is an active scan ability.

Sensor Sweep Rules:
- Roll the linked skill as an ability check
- Final scan range = Base Range + (Successes x Range Per Success)
- Sensor Sweep ignores walls and normal line of sight
- Sensor Sweep checks all tokens in range, not just hidden ones
- Smoke blocks the sweep completely

Smoke blocks Sensor Sweep in any of these cases:
- the scanning unit is inside smoke
- the target is inside smoke
- the scan line between scanner and target crosses smoke

Sensor Sweep Results:
- Any token in range and not blocked by smoke is detected
- Hidden targets are revealed by the sweep
- Visible targets are still valid contacts and may be pinged or called out by the GM or VTT
- Tokens blocked by smoke are not detected

------------------------------------------------------------
18. FLIGHT PACK
------------------------------------------------------------

Flight Pack is a movement ability for suits equipped with experimental thrusters or jump systems.

Flight Pack Rules:
- Roll the linked skill as an ability check
- On 1 or more successes, Flight works normally
- On 0 successes, Flight does not resolve cleanly; roll 1d10 on the Flight Mishap Table
- Flight mishaps are disruptive and tactical, but not meant to be instantly deadly on their own

Flight Mishap Table (1d10):
1. Overheat Warning
   Jets sputter and cut out. Flight fails, the trooper stays grounded, and cannot use Flight again until next turn.
2. Spin Out
   The suit yaws sideways. Drift 1d6 squares in a random direction, then land off-balance.
3. Hard Landing
   The trooper gets airborne but crashes down early. Move only half distance, then lose 1 action next turn.
4. Thruster Flare
   A bright exhaust bloom gives away the trooper's position. They are easy to spot until the end of the round.
5. Smoke Belch
   The pack belches smoke and soot. Place a small smoke patch around the trooper until the end of the round.
6. Power Drain
   The suit browns out. The trooper cannot Aim or Brace until next turn.
7. Wild Burst
   The thrusters kick too hard. The trooper overshoots the landing point by a short distance and ends exposed.
8. Gyro Failure
   The stabilizers glitch. The trooper lands where intended but ends prone or unstable.
9. Comms Screech
   The suit screams static across local comms. Nearby allies are distracted, but nobody is harmed.
10. Control Lock
   Flight fails completely. The trooper must manually reset the pack before trying again.

------------------------------------------------------------
19. TACTICAL ACTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

AIM
- Costs 1 action
- The acting trooper gains +2d6 to their next attack roll
- This affects the attack roll only, not the damage roll
- The effect ends after that one attack

BRACE
- Costs 1 action
- The acting trooper doubles their next defense roll
- Example: 2d6 becomes 4d6
- The effect ends after that one defense roll

NANO REPAIR
- Nano Repair restores armor integrity instead of affecting attack or defense
- Exact repair values, limits, and uses are set by the ability or gear profile using it
- Nano Repair affects Integrity, not Health, unless a special rule says otherwise

------------------------------------------------------------
20. TEMPORARY ROLL MODIFIERS
------------------------------------------------------------

Some effects grant bonus or penalty dice to a unit's next roll.
These may come from command abilities, tactical support, GM effects, environmental hazards, or macros in a VTT.

Temporary next-roll modifiers may affect:
- attack rolls
- defense rolls
- skill rolls
- attribute rolls
- ability rolls

Temporary next-roll modifiers do not affect damage rolls unless a specific rule says otherwise.

If the next roll is an attack:
- the modifier applies to the attack roll only
- it does not apply to the damage roll

If a modifier reduces a roll to 0 dice:
- the roll is still resolved
- the result is an automatic fail or automatic miss
- the modifier is consumed normally

Unless a specific ability says otherwise, a next-roll modifier is consumed after one qualifying roll.

------------------------------------------------------------
21. COMMAND DICE
------------------------------------------------------------

Command Dice represent battlefield leadership, coordination, timing, and support.

Command Dice Rules:
- The commander targets 1 allied token
- The commander spends X Command Dice
- The targeted ally gains +Xd6 to their next roll
- If that next roll is an attack, the bonus applies to the attack roll only, not the damage roll
- The bonus is consumed after that one qualifying roll

Command Dice are intentionally flexible and may be used to support attack, defense, skill, or ability rolls.

------------------------------------------------------------
22. DRONES
------------------------------------------------------------

Some troopers deploy drones.
Drones operate as separate battlefield units controlled by the player or GM.

A drone may have its own:
- Attack Dice
- Defense Dice
- Armor Tier
- Integrity or Health
- Speed
- weapon profile
- special ability

Unless a specific drone rule says otherwise, drones follow the same combat rules as other units.

------------------------------------------------------------
23. GROUP ATTACKS (GM / VTT PLAY AID)
------------------------------------------------------------

For large groups of minor enemies, the GM may resolve same-type NPC attacks as a grouped batch for speed.

Group Attack Rules:
- All selected attackers must be the same NPC type or actor setup
- All selected attackers use the same weapon for that batch
- The group declares 1 target
- Each selected attacker still resolves its own attack under the hood
- The group attack is a speed tool, not a different damage system
- The GM or VTT may mark those attackers as having acted until the next round

This rule is meant to speed up minion turns without changing the underlying combat math.

------------------------------------------------------------
24. EXAMPLE ATTACK
------------------------------------------------------------

Nyx fires a Heavy Rifle at a Tawny trooper in Combat Armor.

Attack Roll:
3 Attack Dice -> 2 successes
Result: +1 damage die

Weapon Damage:
Heavy Rifle base damage = 4d6
With +1 damage die = 5d6

Defense Roll:
Target rolls 2 Defense Dice -> 1 success
Remove 1 damage die

Final Damage Pool:
4d6

Damage Roll:
4 + 6 + 3 + 5 = 18

Armor Interaction:
Heavy weapon vs Medium armor = x2 damage
18 x 2 = 36 damage

Apply Damage:
Damage is applied to Integrity first, then Health.

25. DESIGN GOALS
------------------------------------------------------------

Fast Combat
Minimal bookkeeping and quick resolution.

Tactical Decisions
Positioning, armor tier, weapon choice, defense, support actions, smoke placement, and battlefield scanning all matter.

Explosive Sci-Fi Action
Heavy weapons are terrifying and battlefield movement matters.

VTT Friendly
The system is built for smooth play in Roll20, Foundry, and other digital tabletops.
